OpenMouse turns a physical mouse or trackball into a way to control your Android
device, for people who find the touchscreen hard to use: tremors, limited fine
motor control, or conditions like cerebral palsy.

A large cross-hair cursor follows your mouse. Hold it still and it clicks for you
(dwell click), or press the mouse button. An on-screen menu adds double-tap,
long-press, drag, swipe, and scroll, plus shortcuts for Back, Home, Recent apps,
Notifications, and Quick Settings. A settings screen lets you tune the dwell
time, movement tolerance, cursor size and color, and menu side; changes apply
instantly.

OpenMouse is a clean-room, open-source reimplementation of the closed-source Ease
Mouse, built as a standard Android accessibility service. It needs no root and no
special hardware. On Android 14 and newer it observes the mouse without capturing
your touchscreen, so finger touch keeps working normally.

Private by design: OpenMouse collects no data, contains no ads or trackers, and
never connects to the internet.
